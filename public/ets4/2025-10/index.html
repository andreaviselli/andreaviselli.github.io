Why Bonds Fail Differently? Explainable Multimodal Learning for Multi-Class Default Prediction

Link to working paper ↗

  • Authors: Yi Lu, Aifan Ling, Chaoqun Wang, Yaxin Xu
  • Source: NEP-FOR (2025-09-13)
  • Summary: The paper presents EMDLOT, a novel framework for multi-class bond default prediction that integrates numerical time-series data and unstructured textual data. It employs Time-Aware LSTM to manage irregular sequences and enhances interpretability through soft clustering and multi-level attention mechanisms. The framework demonstrates superior performance in predicting bond defaults compared to traditional and deep learning models, making it a practical tool for financial risk modeling and decision-making.

 

Out-of-sample gravity predictions and trade policy counterfactuals

Link to working paper ↗

  • Authors: Nicolas Apfel, Holger Breinlich, Nick Green, Dennis Novy, J. M. C. Santos Silva, Tom Zylkin
  • Source: NEP-FOR (2025-09-13)
  • Summary: This paper evaluates the out-of-sample predictive power of gravity equations used in trade policy analysis, comparing them with machine learning methods like random forests and neural networks. It finds that while the 3-way gravity model performs well on average, ensemble machine learning methods can outperform it for predicting individual bilateral trade flows. The focus on predictive performance and methodology suggests a strong relevance to forecasting economic time series, particularly in the context of trade flows.

 

Stabilising Lifetime PD Models under Forecast Uncertainty

Link to working paper ↗

  • Authors: Vahab Rostampour
  • Source: NEP-FOR (2025-09-13)
  • Summary: The paper addresses the challenge of estimating lifetime probabilities of default (PDs) under IFRS 9 and CECL by reformulating the forecasting problem within a state-space framework. It introduces an anchored observation model that incorporates a neutral long-run economic state, which stabilizes the PD term structure and reduces forecast noise. The empirical results from simulations indicate that this approach leads to smoother and more interpretable projections, highlighting its potential for improving economic forecasting in credit risk assessment.

 

Meta-Learning Neural Process for Implied Volatility Surfaces with SABR-induced Priors

Link to working paper ↗

  • Authors: Jirong Zhuang, Xuan Wu
  • Source: NEP-FOR (2025-09-13)
  • Summary: The paper presents a novel approach to constructing implied volatility surfaces (IVS) using a meta-learning framework, specifically through a Volatility Neural Process. This model is trained to reconstruct IVS from limited market quotes, significantly improving predictive accuracy over traditional methods like SABR and Gaussian Process. The results indicate a substantial reduction in RMSE, particularly in areas with sparse data, showcasing its potential for practical applications in financial forecasting.

 

Financial Shocks and the Output Growth Distribution

Link to working paper ↗

  • Authors: Francois-Michel Boire, Thibaut Duprey, Alexander Ueberfeldt
  • Source: NEP-FOR (2025-09-13)
  • Summary: This paper introduces a quantile-augmented vector autoregression (QAVAR) model that integrates quantile regressions into a structural VAR framework, allowing for flexible, nonparametric forecasts of conditional moments and tail risk measures for GDP. The findings reveal that adverse financial shocks significantly increase downside risk to GDP growth, highlighting the model’s capability to capture nonlinearities and heterogeneous tail dynamics in economic forecasting. The QAVAR model’s innovative approach to forecasting tail risks and conditional moments makes it a valuable tool for predicting economic outcomes under financial stress.

 

The ECB-Multi Country Model. A semi-structural model for forecasting and policy analysis for the largest euro area countries

Link to working paper ↗

  • Authors: Angelini, Elena, Bokan, Nikola, Ciccarelli, Matteo, Lalik, Magdalena, Zimic, Srečko
  • Source: NEP-FOR (2025-09-13)
  • Summary: The paper presents the European Central Bank’s Multi Country model (ECB-MC), which is designed for economic forecasting and policy analysis for major euro area economies. It emphasizes the model’s ability to conduct scenario, risk, and sensitivity analyses, thereby providing a structured approach to forecasting economic dynamics. The ECB-MC serves as a reference for macroeconomic projections, highlighting its practical applications in understanding the effects of economic shocks.

 

Assessing Asymmetric Macroeconomic Risk

Link to working paper ↗

  • Authors: Stéphane Lhuissier
  • Source: NEP-FOR (2025-10-13)
  • Summary: The paper introduces a dynamic factor model with time-varying skewness to assess asymmetric macroeconomic risk, specifically focusing on the predictive capabilities of macroeconomic aggregates. It demonstrates that the model enhances the detection of downside risks to growth and provides more accurate predictive distributions, particularly during economic downturns. The findings suggest that utilizing a richer information set can significantly improve economic forecasting accuracy.

 

 

Adaptive Temporal Fusion Transformers for Cryptocurrency Price Prediction

Link to working paper ↗

  • Authors: Arash Peik, Mohammad Ali Zare Chahooki, Amin Milani Fard, Mehdi Agha Sarram
  • Source: NEP-FOR (2025-09-13)
  • Summary: This paper presents an adaptive Temporal Fusion Transformer (TFT) approach for short-term cryptocurrency price prediction, addressing the challenges posed by market volatility and non-stationarity. The authors introduce a novel segmentation method that captures significant upward price movements, allowing for more responsive forecasting. Experimental results indicate that this adaptive model outperforms traditional fixed-length TFT and LSTM models in terms of prediction accuracy and trading profitability, showcasing its potential for practical applications in trading strategies.
  • Reason for Interest: The adaptive segmentation and pattern-based forecasting methods could be applied to economic time series, such as stock prices or commodity prices, to enhance prediction accuracy in volatile markets.
